Raw UGC Final Expense The $22 Decision FE-UGC-01
Character: 61yo Black man, warm Southern accent · Back porch, evening · Handheld camera, warm lamp light
Director Notes75% energy. Handheld with slight movement. Let the lamp light be warm. Don't rush anything.
HOOK
"I put this off for eight years. Eight years."
↳ [BEAT] Let that land. Slight headshake. Hold 1.5 seconds before continuing.
BODY
"My mother passed last year. Didn't have anything in place. Me and my two sisters had to scramble. I'm not letting my kids go through that."
BRIDGE
"A friend at church told me about this. Twenty-two dollars a month. No medical questions. I applied on my phone in about nine minutes."
CLOSE
"Rate's locked. Permanent. My family is taken care of. Twenty-two dollars."
CTA
"Link's right there. Do it tonight."
Performance NoteDon't rush the "eight years" pause. The headshake does more than the words. The church reference adds community trust — keep it.

CTA
"Link is right there. Run the comparison. Do it right now if you want."
Performance NoteThe parking lot spontaneity is the whole ad. The "I'm literally sitting here" line should feel genuinely stunned, not performed.

DR Hook Medicare The Original Medicare Trap MEDICARE-DR-01
Character: 65yo white man, calm authority, professional delivery · 70% energy, measured pace
Director Notes70% energy. Measured. Let every stat breathe. This is calm authority — not alarm. The numbers do the alarming.
HOOK
"Original Medicare has no out-of-pocket maximum. None. One hospital stay can cost you eleven thousand dollars and Medicare won't stop the billing."
EXPAND
"Most people don't find this out until they're already in the hospital."
SOLUTION
"Medicare Advantage plans have out-of-pocket maximums, often include dental and vision, and the premium can be zero dollars a month."
CLOSE
"One free call to a licensed advisor. They're paid by the carrier not by you. Zero pressure."
CTA
"Number's in the link. Call before open enrollment closes."
Performance NoteThe "$11,000" stat is the hook. Make sure it's the first number they hear. "Paid by the carrier not by you" kills the sales objection before it forms.
DR Hook ACA Health The COBRA Trap ACA-DR-01
Character: 36yo woman, direct, slightly frustrated energy · 85% energy, close-up medium shot, slight urgency
Director Notes85% energy. Close-up medium shot. The word "Stop" should feel like grabbing someone's arm. She's doing them a favor by interrupting them.
HOOK
"If you just lost your job and you're about to sign up for COBRA — stop."
DATA
"COBRA averages $617 a month for a single person. The ACA marketplace for that same person with that income might be twenty to forty dollars."
BRIDGE
"You have 63 days after losing employer coverage to enroll in the marketplace. That clock is running."
CTA
"Calculator is in the link. Run your numbers before you sign that COBRA paperwork."
Performance Note"Stop" is the first word. It should feel like grabbing someone's arm. The 63-day deadline is urgency without being fake — use it.

Before / After Final Expense Before My Mom Passed / After FE-BA-01
Character: Any adult 45–65 · Direct to camera, home setting · 75% energy, quiet urgency
Director NotesThe BEFORE should feel specific and normal, not dramatic. The TURN is the pivot — deliver it with weight. The AFTER should feel quiet relief, not celebration.
BEFORE
"A year ago I hadn't thought about it. My mom was fine. We were fine."
THE TURN
"She passed in March. Eleven thousand dollars. We had ten days."
↳ [PAUSE] Let those three sentences land separately.
AFTER
"I got covered the same month. Twenty dollars. Rate locked. My kids will never have ten days like we had."
CTA
"Link below. Nine minutes."
Performance Note"We had ten days" is the most powerful phrase in FE creative. The brevity of the TURN is intentional — don't elaborate. Let the viewer fill it in.

DR Hook ACA Health The Gig Worker Subsidy ACA-DR-02
Character: 32yo man, gig economy worker (Uber/DoorDash coded) · Car interior or casual home · 90% energy, informational urgency
Director Notes90% energy. This is the gig worker version of the COBRA trap — same urgency, different audience. Targets the massive uninsured gig economy segment. Very high-frequency angle.
HOOK
"If you drive for Uber, DoorDash, Instacart — any of those — and you don't have health insurance, I need sixty seconds."
DATA
"Gig workers who make under $60,000 a year typically qualify for ACA subsidies that bring premiums down to under $50 a month. Sometimes zero."
EXPAND
"You are classified as self-employed. That's the trigger. The marketplace was literally designed for you."
SOLUTION
"Thirty-second income calculator. Tells you your subsidy instantly. No sales call. No obligation."
CTA
"Link below. You're probably leaving money on the table right now."
Performance NoteVery high frequency, very wide targeting. "You are classified as self-employed. That's the trigger." — this reframe is the entire script. It's educational DR, not emotional DR.
