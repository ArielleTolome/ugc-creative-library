Core Message
The scramble is preventable. $22/month, no exam, no questions. Get it done so your family never goes through what you went through.
Hook Type
DR Hook — opens with the loss event, converts to resolution
Format
Raw UGC — handheld, no production lighting, authentic setting
Character
Age: 47 | Ethnicity: Black | Accent: Warm Georgia
Wardrobe
Casual weekend clothes — henley or flannel, nothing polished
Location
Front porch steps, evening light, slightly overcast. No props except phone in pocket.
Energy Level
60% — calm, reflective, not performing. Pacing is unhurried.
Camera Direction
Handheld static, medium shot, slightly downward gaze allowed
Dialogue
"My mom passed in February. There was nothing in place. Me and my siblings had to figure it out fast. I had mine locked in by March. Twenty dollars a month. No questions. I just wasn't going to be that conversation for my kids." [pause, direct look to camera] "Link's below."

Section 2⚡ Rapid Brief Format
One-line format · Fast production days
Rapid Brief — One Line, One Concept
For fast production days when you're shooting 5–10 concepts and don't need the full standard brief. One line contains everything talent needs. Format: [age] [ethnicity] [gender] [accent] [wardrobe] [location] [energy]% [camera] saying "[hook] [offer] [CTA]"
When to UseUse Rapid Briefs when: shooting multiple concepts in one session, working with experienced talent who only needs the beat, or generating batches through AI tools. Always upgrade to Standard Brief for new concepts with unfamiliar talent.

Section 3📊 Batch Brief Format
Table format · 5+ concepts in parallel
Batch Brief — Monday Morning Production
When you're briefing 5+ concepts at once for a production batch. Abbreviated fields in table format. Each row is one concept. Use for Monday morning briefing sessions where the team needs to see the full week's lineup at a glance.
When to UseBatch briefs work best when: all concepts share a production day, talent is already booked, and you need rapid comparison across concepts to ensure variety in angles, verticals, and character demographics. Upgrade any winning concept to a Standard Brief before re-shooting.

Section 4🔄 Revision Brief Format
Iteration format · Changing one variable at a time
Revision Brief — Iterate on What's Working
When a concept is performing but you want to test a variation. The revision brief references the parent concept and specifies only what changes. Single-variable testing — change one thing, measure the difference. Never revise more than 2 elements at once or you lose attribution.
When to UseUse Revision Briefs when: a concept has proven performance data, you want to test a specific hypothesis (hook swap, character swap, CTA change), or you're in the iteration phase of the creative loop with Marcus. Always keep the parent concept running as control.
PARENT: STD-FE-001 Revision A — Swap Character Gender + Age
Parent Concept ID
STD-FE-001 · FE Grief-to-Action · 47yo Black man, Georgia porch
What Performed (Keep)
Hook structure (loss → personal action → rate reveal → family protection). CTA placement. Raw UGC format. 60% energy level. Evening porch setting.
What to Change
Character: swap from 47yo Black man → 55yo white woman. Accent: Georgia → Midwest. Setting stays porch but now morning light instead of evening.
Hypothesis
The hook structure works across demographics. Female character may unlock a different audience segment (women 50–65) who over-index on FE consideration but under-convert. Morning light may feel more "fresh start" vs evening's reflective tone.
New Dialogue Beats
"My sister passed in October. Nothing in place. I watched our family scramble for two weeks. I had mine locked by November. Twenty dollars a month. No questions. My kids won't go through what we went through. Link below."
Expected Improvement
Expand demographic reach without losing hook effectiveness. Target: 15%+ new audience overlap with same or better CVR.
PARENT: STD-AUTO-001 Revision B — Swap Hook from Comedic to Anger
Parent Concept ID
STD-AUTO-001 · Auto Loyalty Penalty · 41yo Black man, comedic delivery
What Performed (Keep)
Core angle (loyalty penalty), savings amount ($94/mo), "seven minutes" speed frame, same coverage proof point. Direct-to-camera format.
What to Change
Emotional register: swap comedic (90% energy, loyalty card bit) → frustrated/angry (85% energy, "I was angry"). Character: 41yo Black man → 48yo Black woman, Chicago. Drop the coffee shop comparison entirely — replace with rate increase frustration.
Hypothesis
Rate increase anger is a stronger conversion driver than humor for audiences who just received their renewal. Testing whether frustration-to-empowerment arc outperforms humor-to-action arc on same savings message.
New Dialogue Beats
"When I got my renewal I was angry. Third year in a row. Same car. Better driver. Rate went up. I ran the comparison angry. I switched angry. I'm saving $94 a month and I'm still a little angry — but now it's at myself for waiting this long. Link below."
Expected Improvement
Higher CTR from audiences in renewal window (Q1/Q3). Test against parent for 7 days minimum before scaling.
PARENT: STD-ACA-001 Revision C — Platform Swap: TikTok POV Format
Parent Concept ID
STD-ACA-001 · ACA Self-Employed Creator · 26yo Black woman, studio
What Performed (Keep)
Self-employed creator identity. "Stop waiting" CTA urgency. $19/month proof point. Creator-audience relatability. 85% energy.
What to Change
Format: swap Branded Lifestyle → TikTok POV. Camera: medium shot → close selfie, direct eye contact. Opening: swap "Year three of doing this" → "POV: You just went full-time and you still don't have insurance." Second-person address instead of first-person testimonial.
Hypothesis
TikTok POV format drives higher completion rate (viewer feels directly addressed vs. observing someone else's story). Second-person framing may increase urgency-to-click for viewers currently in the "just went full-time" moment.
New Dialogue Beats
"POV: You went full-time six months ago and you still don't have health insurance because you keep saying 'I'll figure it out when I'm making more.' ACA marketplace. Nineteen dollars a month. It took me nine minutes. You have nine minutes. Link below. Stop waiting."
Expected Improvement
Higher completion rate on TikTok specifically (+20% target). Test with same audience targeting as parent to isolate format variable.
